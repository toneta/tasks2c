About

Accurate Home Data was developed to help clarify discrepancies in a home’s living area given by local municipalities, real estate agents, or homeowners. The result is a more accurate representation of a home’s square footage. It helps reduce errors and misrepresentation as well as avoiding potential problems resulting from inaccurate information.

What’s Involved?

There are two types of reports; exterior only, which limits access to the property to perimeter of the exterior of the house and the interior/exterior, which offers more detail.

Exterior:

Our exterior report calculates a home’s living area by measuring the exterior of the house and including all heated, above grade living area. This will determine the home’s GLA (gross living area). All attempts are made to ensure accurate living space; however, exterior only measurements may mistakenly include and/or exclude some living area.

Interior/Exterior:

Our interior/exterior report includes the exterior measurements as well as an interior walk-through of the house. This helps determine a more accurate square footage of a home and includes room locations. Also, a walk-through of the basement can be done, if requested, to determine the size of the basement and estimate any finished areas. Below grade basement finishes are not included in the gross living area.

All measurements and reports are completed by, or supervised by, state licensed or certified residential appraisers. Measurements are done to accepted or mandated local standards. Measurements are considered reliable; however, because of variances in weather, measuring devices, and physical obstructions, they are not guaranteed.
